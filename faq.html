What's the Problem?

Traffic is extremely congested around campus since we rely on one main route to get in, out, and around campus. By trying to increase the flow on Ferst Drive, we hope that we can reduce traffic.

How do we plan on solving this?

This is how we differentiate ourselves from other parties who tried to solve this problem. We don't focus on trying to reduce cars on the road or promote other modes of transportation because that is just avoiding the underlying issue, not solving it. We plan to tackle this problem by controlling pedestrian traffic and how often they cross roads.

What are some pre-existing solutions?

There are multiple solutions to control traffic congestion. First, we can install actual traffic lights. This, however, is very costly and not desired because the school does not need more traffic lights most of the time. Second, we can have police officers or contruction workers physically monitor the traffic. This is currently the most frequently used solution because workers can directky see and make decisions that can aid the traffic flow real-time. However, the percentage for error will drastically increase as humans try to cover more area to control the traffic with more people. They are prone to mistakes and cannot work long hours. Finally, there are many companies offering "portable" traffic lights. These are expensive, often just for rental, and highly complicated to set-up/configure/monitor. They are also not very portable since the products are big, bulky, and require a lot of work for installation.

How much of this project is implemented right now?

Currently we are just out of the design phase and into the implementation phase. We have a testing "robot," web applcation, network program, and a server. We are currently working on the algorithm, device communication, and the actual robots.

How is Traffi-Bot different to other portable traffic lights?

Traffi-Bot is different because all the traffic configuration will not require contant human assistance. It can determine the traffic flow real-time and choose the best algorithm to aid the flow. It is also communicative; meaning, it will "talk" with other traffi-bots to make fluid traffic algorithm as an entire system.

Who are our partners?

We are working with GTPD to figure out what are the limitations and posibilities of our project. They have not only guided us towards the right direction, but have also influenced some of our design choices. We are also trying to work with the department of transportation to allow testing in real enviornment.
