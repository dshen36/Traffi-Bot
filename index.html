Our Purpose

Traffic has always been a prevalent problem at Georgia Tech. Local commuters would tell you that the time to leave campus ALONE would take about 15 minutes. This is specifically due to a combination of classes all ending at the same time, the myriad of professors and students leaving the campus, and the pedestrians crossing at will.

Others have attempted to solve this problem by creating car-pooling apps, projecting average traffic times, or initializing a community bike program. However, we approached this issue differently by focusing one of the larger underlying issues on campus; the unorganized and unrestrained way that pedestrians cross the road.
